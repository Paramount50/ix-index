Summary

One paragraph. State what this RFC proposes in concrete terms a reader can act on. If the summary is hard to write, the RFC is not ready yet.

Motivation

Why does this need to exist? What goes wrong today? What forces the change? Cite specific files, options, conversations, or incidents. Prefer concrete pain over abstract elegance.

Detailed design

The bulk of the document. Describe the proposed shape in enough detail that someone who is not the author could implement it. Cover:

  • The user-visible API: options, commands, file layout.
  • The internal mechanics where they affect more than one file.
  • How this composes with existing pieces: modules, fleet helpers, lint rules, image conventions.
  • Migration: what existing callers or configs need to change, and whether that is an automatic rewrite or manual.

Use real names and real types. Avoid abstract sketches. Concrete is reviewable; vague is not.

Drawbacks

What goes wrong if we ship this? Performance cost, increased surface area, lock-in, operator confusion, security implications. Be honest. Listing zero drawbacks means the section was skipped.

Alternatives

What else was considered? Why is the proposed design preferred over each? Include the "do nothing" alternative explicitly.

Prior art

Where does this pattern exist already, in this repo, in NixOS, in upstream Linux, in cloud providers, in other RFC processes? Link concrete artifacts where possible.

Unresolved questions

Specific open points the RFC author wants reviewers to answer or push back on. Keep them tractable: each should be resolvable inside the PR thread, not its own follow-up RFC.

Future possibilities

Optional. Sketch directions this design enables but does not commit to. Useful for reviewers to understand "where does this go next" without thinking the RFC is promising to deliver it.
